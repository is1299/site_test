Multimodal AI combining EHR and CT imaging to predict cardiovascular disease risk in breast cancer patients.

Background

Women are at a high risk of mortality from both cardiovascular disease and breast cancer. However, heart disease remains the leading cause of premature death of women in Canada [1]. The National Lung Cancer Screening Trial (NLST) in the US found that 356 patients died of lung cancer; however, 486 patients died due to cardiovascular disease (CVD) [2]. Numerous studies have shown that cancer survivors are more likely to develop and die from CVD than the general population. Thus, making screening for co-morbidities like CVD is essential to lower the mortality rate. The CT simulation scans obtained for radiotherapy planning for breast cancer offer an opportunity for simultaneous CVD risk estimation in at-risk patients.

Aim of the Project

The overarching aim of this research is to improve early prediction of cardiovascular disease (CVD) mortality in breast cancer patients through the application of computer vision and natural language processing integration. While traditional risk models such as the Framingham Risk Score serve as clinical benchmarks, they often fall short in delivering the detailed insights necessary for individualized evaluation in oncology settings. This work investigates novel deep learning techniques to address this gap, focusing on both CT imaging and electronic medical record (EMR) data, and multimodal approaches to combine the two and provide a final mortality risk prediction much like a clinician would.

Data

Data for this project were accessed with the approval of the BC Cancer- UBC Research Ethics Board (REB) (Certificate #H16−01792). The complete data set includes computed tomography (CT) scans from 29,057 patients who underwent breast cancer screening at BC Cancer, as well as electronic medical record (EMR) data from 67,821 cancer patients collected until 2019 at the BC Cancer clinics.
